# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

---

## Our Standards

### Positive Behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- **Being Respectful** - Showing empathy and kindness toward others
- **Being Collaborative** - Working together effectively
- **Being Constructive** - Providing and accepting constructive feedback
- **Being Professional** - Maintaining professionalism in all interactions
- **Being Inclusive** - Welcoming diverse perspectives and experiences
- **Being Supportive** - Helping others learn and grow
- **Being Accountable** - Taking responsibility for mistakes

### Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- **Harassment** - Offensive comments, trolling, or personal attacks
- **Discrimination** - Prejudice based on protected characteristics
- **Intimidation** - Threatening or bullying behavior
- **Privacy Violations** - Publishing private information without consent
- **Sexual Attention** - Unwelcome sexual advances or comments
- **Disruption** - Sustained disruption of discussions or events
- **Spam** - Unsolicited advertising or self-promotion

---

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, and also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces. Examples of representing our community include:

- Using an official project email address
- Posting via an official social media account
- Acting as an appointed representative at events
- Contributing to project repositories
- Participating in project discussions

## Reporting

### How to Report

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it by contacting:

**Email**: conduct@phoenixvc.co.za

All reports will be handled with discretion and confidentiality.

### What to Include

When reporting, please provide:

1. **Your contact information** - For follow-up
2. **Names of involved parties** - Including witnesses
3. **Description of incident** - What happened
4. **Date and time** - When it occurred
5. **Context** - Where it happened (issue, PR, discussion, etc.)
6. **Additional information** - Screenshots, links, etc.

### What to Expect

- **Acknowledgment**: Within 24 hours
- **Investigation**: Within 7 days
- **Resolution**: Based on severity
- **Follow-up**: Status updates throughout

---

## Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these guidelines in determining consequences:

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or unprofessional behavior.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved for a specified period. This includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period. No public or private interaction with the people involved is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment, or aggression.

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community.

## Examples

### Acceptable Interactions

**Code Review Comments:**

- "This approach works, but consider using X pattern for better performance. Here's an example..."
- "I noticed a potential security issue here. Could we add input validation?"
- "Great work! One suggestion: we could simplify this by..."

**Issue Discussions:**

- "I'm experiencing the same issue. Here's my environment details..."
- "This is a duplicate of #123. Let's continue the discussion there."
- "I disagree with this approach because... What do you think about...?"

### Unacceptable Interactions

**Code Review Comments:**

- "This code is terrible. Did you even test this?"
- "Only an idiot would write code like this."
- "Go learn to code before submitting PRs."

**Issue Discussions:**

- "This is a stupid idea."
- "You clearly don't know what you're talking about."
- Personal attacks or insults

## Best Practices

### For Contributors

1. **Assume Good Intent** - Give others the benefit of the doubt
2. **Ask Questions** - Seek to understand before responding
3. **Be Patient** - Not everyone has the same experience level
4. **Stay On Topic** - Keep discussions focused and relevant
5. **Admit Mistakes** - We all make them; learn and move on
6. **Give Credit** - Acknowledge others' contributions

### For Maintainers

1. **Lead by Example** - Model the behavior you want to see
2. **Be Responsive** - Address issues promptly
3. **Be Fair** - Apply rules consistently
4. **Be Transparent** - Communicate decisions clearly
5. **Be Supportive** - Help contributors succeed
6. **Be Decisive** - Take action when needed

---

## Building a Positive Community

### Communication Guidelines

**Be Clear**
- Use simple, direct language
- Provide context and examples
- Break down complex topics

**Be Respectful**
- Use inclusive language
- Avoid assumptions
- Respect different perspectives

**Be Helpful**
- Share knowledge generously
- Point to resources
- Offer constructive solutions

### Conflict Resolution

When disagreements arise:

1. **Step Back** - Take time to cool down
2. **Seek Understanding** - Ask clarifying questions
3. **Find Common Ground** - Focus on shared goals
4. **Propose Solutions** - Offer constructive alternatives
5. **Escalate if Needed** - Involve maintainers when necessary
